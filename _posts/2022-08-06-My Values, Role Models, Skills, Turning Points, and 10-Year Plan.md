I recently watched a [short video clip](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/qvss8ZzuwCg) by Chloe Shih on a team bonding exercise called the personal shield. I thought it was a great way to do some self-reflection, and I figured I might as well share my own personal shield on the blog. 

I didn’t go into too much detail on each bullet point, but I’m hoping it’ll inspire you to do the exercise for yourself!

# Role Models
**Stephen Dubner**  
*Known for:* Co-author of Freakonomics, hosting the Freakanomics podcast, co-hosting the No Stupid Questions podcast  
*Role model for:* Communication  

I admire Stephen's ability to challenge guests and his co-host without making them uncomfortable, as well as his ability to communicate complex ideas using simple language. I would love to emulate his communication style.

-

**Ramit Sethi**  
*Known for:* Author of [I Will Teach You To Be Rich](https://www.amazon.com/Will-Teach-You-Be-Rich/dp/0761147489), created [iwillteachyoutoberich.com](https://www.iwillteachyoutoberich.com/)   
*Role model for:* Growth mindset, self-improvement  

Ramit's content is incredibly motivating. Ramit talks a lot about overcoming invisible scripts--ideas influenced by our environment that we don’t even realize control our behavior. He models the growth mindset incredibly well. He encourages you to live your best life by working for it (buy all the lattes you want!) while providing insightful and actionable advice.

-

**William MacAskill**  
*Known for:* Founder of [Centre of Effective Altruism](https://www.centreforeffectivealtruism.org/), [80000hours](https://80000hours.org/), and [Giving What We Can](https://www.givingwhatwecan.org/)  
*Role model for:* Philosophy, movement-building  

Although MacAskill didn’t necessarily come up with all the main ideas of EA, he popularized it and was the driving force behind several institutions that converted the philosophy of effective altruism into action. These organizations introduced me to ideas that expanded my personal circle of caring, and they have significantly influenced my plans for the future.

-

I also have a few PM-related role models, but perhaps that would be better suited for another post.

# Turning Points
**Working at a startup early on**  
Led me to lean towards bigger companies for first job after college. Gave me a taste of what it’s like working at a seed-stage company. At the startup, I had independence and ownership but very little mentorship or guidance.


**Googling Warren Buffett**  
Kickstarted my interest in personal finance.


**Studying abroad in Japan**  
Gave me a new perspective on life, escaping the rat race, prioritizing relationships with people / friendships, exposed me to new ideas and types of people.


**Gap semester in Taiwan**  
Made me more connected with my family, culture, and language. Helped me become more comfortable with being by myself.

# 10 Years From Now (Age 32)
**Career**  
Be a product leader working on challenging and interesting problems.

**Entrepreneurship**  
Have my own business where I work on problems I’m personally interested in.

**Finances**  
Be very financially comfortable, or even financially independent. At least LeanFI.

**Lifestyle**  
Have the flexibility to live and work from wherever I want.

**Relationships**  
Have fulfilling and satisfying relationships with my friends, family, and romantic partner.

**Society**  
Contributing to social causes I care strongly about with my money and/or time.
